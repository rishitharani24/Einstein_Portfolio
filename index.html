About Me

I am Albert Einstein, a theoretical physicist born in 1879 in Ulm, Germany, best known for developing the theory of relativity, which transformed our understanding of space, time, and gravity. My work in physics, especially my explanation of the photoelectric effect, a key contribution to quantum theory, earned me the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921, and my famous equation, E=mc², continues to shape modern scientific thought. Beyond my scientific achievements, I have always been a passionate advocate for social justice, civil rights, and global peace, believing in the power of science to enhance and uplift humanity. Learn more about me on my Wikipedia page.

Published Papers

Does the Inertia of a Body Depend Upon Its Energy Content?

In this paper, I proposed that the inertia of a body is directly related to its energy content, leading to the famous equation E = mc², which links mass and energy.

The Foundation of the General Theory of Relativity

In this paper, I developed the general theory of relativity, which describes gravity not as a force, but as the curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy.

On the Electrodynamics of Moving Bodies

Here I proposed the special theory of relativity, changing our understanding of space and time by showing that they are relative to the observer's motion.
